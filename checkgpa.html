Semester Performance

A student's performance in a semester is calculated as Grade Point Aggregate (GPA). This involves the awarding of credit points in respect of each course taken during the semester. To this end, numerical values are attached to the letter grades above as follows:

  • A -> 5
  • B -> 4
  • C -> 3
  • D -> 2
  • E -> 1
  • F -> 0

So, the semester GPA is obtained as the ratio of total number of credit points (TCP) to the total number of unit (TNU) of courses offered during the semester.
Thus, GPA = TCP/TNU
For Example:
TCP = 26
TNU = 23
Then, GPA = TCP/TNU = 26/23 = 1.13
So, the GPA for such a student that semester is 1.13.
